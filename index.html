Test-Time Scaling in Diffusion LLMs via Hidden Semi-Autoregressive Experts (HEX)

Jihoon Lee1, Hoyeon Moon1, Kevin Zhai5, Arun Kumar Chithanar, Anit Kumar Sahu2, Soummya Kar3, Chul Lee, Souradip Chakraborty4, Amrit Singh Bedi5
1Yonsei University   2Oracle   3Carnegie Mellon University   4University of Maryland, College Park   5University of Central Florida
Preprint 2025
Paper / Project Page Code Data / Eval Scripts Video (coming soon)

HEX: Ensembles semi-autoregressive block schedules and aggregates via majority vote to robustly improve reasoning accuracy without retraining.

Abstract

Diffusion-based large language models (dLLMs) flexibly model extreme dependence in textual data; how to best utilize this at inference time remains open. We uncover that dLLMs implicitly learn a mixture of semi-autoregressive experts, where different generation orders reveal specialized behaviors. Committing to a single fixed schedule collapses performance. We introduce HEX, a training-free inference method that ensembles heterogeneous block schedules and majority-votes outputs, avoiding failure modes of any single schedule. On reasoning benchmarks such as GSM8K, accuracy rises up to 3.56× (24.72%→88.10%), and we observe gains on MATH (16.40%→40.00%), ARC-C (54.18%→87.80%), and TruthfulQA (28.36%→57.46%), establishing a new paradigm for test-time scaling in diffusion LLMs.
